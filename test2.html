Timelines of Jackie Appiah

  • 1983: Birth
    • December 5: Jackie Appiah is born in Toronto, Canada, to Ghanaian parents.
  • 1993: Relocation to Ghana
    • Jackie moves back to Ghana with her family at the age of 10, where she begins her education and grows up.
  • Early 2000s: Early Acting Career
    • Jackie begins her acting career, starting with minor roles. She becomes a household name in Ghanaian and Nigerian (Nollywood) films.
  • 2005: Breakthrough Role
    • Jackie gains major recognition after her role in the TV series "Things We Do for Love," where she plays the character Enyonam. This marks her as a star in the Ghanaian film industry.
  • 2007–2009: Nollywood Success
    • Jackie’s popularity spreads beyond Ghana to Nigeria, where she becomes one of the most sought-after actresses in Nollywood, the Nigerian film industry.
    • She stars in numerous hit Nollywood films, including "Beyoncé: The President’s Daughter" and "Princess Tyra".
